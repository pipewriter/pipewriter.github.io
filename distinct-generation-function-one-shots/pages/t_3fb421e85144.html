How it works

Every point owns a continuous DNA vector. Each gene is a random Fourier feature field — a sum of cosines with random directions, frequencies and phases:

gene(p)=√(2/M)·Σ cos(p·ωᵢ+φᵢ)

Frequencies ωᵢ are drawn from a Gaussian of width 1/ℓ. By Bochner's theorem the field's spatial correlation is the Fourier transform of that spectrum — so DNA decorrelates smoothly with distance. The correlation length ℓ is the "genetic locality" slider.

Nearby points share most genes (similar), distant points share none (foreign) — the smooth drift in between is your "sun-damage". Terrain, color and life are a deterministic genotype→phenotype map of the DNA.

Why it's elegant

Stateless · deterministic · O(1) per point (no neighbors, no storage) · infinite world · one tunable knob (ℓ) controls the entire "how far until alien" feel.
