Spectral Genome Fields

Every point of this infinite world carries a genome — a vector sampled from a Gaussian-process field via Random Fourier Features. By Bochner's theorem the genome similarity between two places is exp(−d²/2ℓ²): neighbors are nearly identical, distant places grow genetically foreign.

Terrain, biome, color, and every tree & rock are decoded from the local genome. The field is stateless, deterministic, and O(1) per sample — fly forever, it never repeats and never loads.
