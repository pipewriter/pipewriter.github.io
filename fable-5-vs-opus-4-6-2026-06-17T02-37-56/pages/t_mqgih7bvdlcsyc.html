HEREDITY FIELD

phylogenetic noise — every place inherits its genome from larger ancestral regions, mutated at each scale. distance apart = generations of divergence.
▸ how it works
Space is covered by nested square cells, 16 km down to 128 m — each scale a generation. A cell's genome is its parent's genome plus a small mutation hashed purely from the cell's coordinates: no storage, no simulation, no history. Rarely a hash crosses a threshold and a sun-damage burst slams a few genes hard, producing a mutant enclave.

The 12-gene genome at any point is expressed into everything you see: uplift, mountain building, terracing, pigment, frost line, flora and its growth form, ground patterning.

Two nearby points share every ancestor but the youngest — familiar, never identical. Distant points diverged generations ago — alien. Every sample is O(1): 8 levels × 4 cells × 12 genes, independent of world size.
