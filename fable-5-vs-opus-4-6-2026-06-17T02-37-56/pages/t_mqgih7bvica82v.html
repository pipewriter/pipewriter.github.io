HOW IT WORKS

Every region is a cell of a jittered lattice. A 16-gene genome is a slowly-drifting random field (correlation length ≈ 16 km) sampled once at the cell's nucleus, plus a tiny per-region mutation, then quantized into the 48-letter A/C/G/T strand above. Terrain shape, palette, snowline, flora and rock are decoded purely from that strand, with parameters blended between neighboring nuclei so the land is seamless. Nearby regions inherit nearly the same strand — a few mutated letters. Distant regions share no ancestry. Stateless, deterministic, O(1) per sample, infinite domain.
