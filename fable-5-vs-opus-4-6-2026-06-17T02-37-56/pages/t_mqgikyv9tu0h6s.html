A ONE-MINUTE MEMORY TRAIL

Before maps, before paper, before writing itself, every human carried the way home in their head. The land was the notebook: a red gate of rock, the ford where water sings, the spiral sign painted by your tribe, the tree the lightning killed. Lose the sequence, lose your life.

Walk this trail once and let the eleven stations carve themselves in, in order — each has its own color, shape, sound, and note on a rising scale. This is a method of loci: later, you can hang your own memories on these eleven hooks.
