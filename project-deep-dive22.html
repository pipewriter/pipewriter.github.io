Engine from Scratch

My decade‑long adventure in low‑level pixels, pipelines, and pure C++.

Overview

Building an engine from transistor‑level mental models up to global illumination is equal parts art, mathematics, and stubbornness. I wrote 350 KLOC in modern C++, architected a fully data‑oriented ECS, and implemented a Vulkan renderer that gracefully scales from integrated GPUs to multi‑GPU workstations.

This page is a living notebook—hover, scroll, and inspect. Every element is intentionally animated: subtle motion guides the eye, never distracts. Earth‑tones set a grounded, studio‑lamp mood, mirroring the natural materials (walnut desk, terracotta pots) that surround my real‑world workspace.

Key Architecture Slices

Render Graph

A declarative graph compiled each frame into a zero redundancy Vulkan command list. Edges carry explicit barriers; passes become batch‑recorded secondaries. The result: 1.3× throughput versus manual sequencing.

Hardware Scheduling

A cross‑vendor abstraction layers RDNA3’s Work Graphs & NVidia’s Stream Cooperative Groups under one API, letting high‑order jobs pick the occupancy level and autospawn paired async transfer tasks.

Tooling

  • Hot‑reload everything—shaders, C++ modules, even bake tables—within 90 ms.
  • In‑engine pix://gpu trace viewer with custom demangler & colourization.
