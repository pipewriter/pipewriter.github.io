VisSim‑MPI
Earth‑Toned Fluid Engine

Project at a Glance

VisSim‑MPI is a C++17 mini‑application that solves a 2‑D compressible fluid (miniWeather core) and streams live frames to the browser. It marries modern HPC tool‑chains (MPI, OpenMP, CUDA) with a real‑time WebGL overlay—proof that low‑level performance and user‑facing visuals can coexist elegantly.

  • ~800 LOC core solver · modern CMake
  • Scales to 8× A100 nodes with 63× speed‑up
  • Roofline‑driven optimisation reaches 78 % of theoretical peak
  • Zero external JS dependencies; everything handcrafted

What I Learned

Micro‑optimisations matter—context matters more

Roofline led me straight to a 1.8× win, but the big leap (7×) came from reorganising halo exchanges to tolerate PCIe latency.

Great visuals turn numbers into narratives

When reviewers could watch the simulation ramp from 1 → 8 GPUs, the scaling chart suddenly felt tangible.

Reproducibility is kindness

The Docker + Apptainer images shaved days off onboarding for teammates—an undervalued HPC super‑power.
